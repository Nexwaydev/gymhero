CROSS CRUNCHES

It's more effective and easy exercise for abs and the oblique muscles. It strengthens the core and strengthens your abdominal muscle. Cross crunches have a lower impact on your body than push-ups or sit-ups, and they can help reduce or manage back pain.

CHEST PRESS

The chest press is one of the key foundational upper body exercises. It is a modification of the bench press, but the chest press swaps out the barbell for dumbbells, requiring you to use more core stability and single-arm control.

DIPS

Dips are another great compound bodyweight exercise to add to your arsenal. They work the chest, back, triceps, shoulders, and core. Maintaining proper form is key to preventing shoulder irritation.

SUSPENDED ROWS

Rows are a staple exercise for your back, upper body, and core. While you can certainly stick to classic bent-over dumbbell rows, bodyweight rows increase the demand on your abs and biceps.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Do I Need To Work Out Everyday?

+

No, you do not need to work out everyday. In fact, in most cases, I would at least 1-2 day of total rest week. However, just because you have a rest or recovery day scheduled into your calender, doesn't necessarily mean you aren't active at all on these days. Light, regular movement such as walking your dog around the block, or taking time for stretching are great recovery techniques to help reduce tension in tight muscles.

—

How Long Does It Take To See Results

+

It can take anywhere from 6-8 weeks or more to see results from your program. That can be discouraging as so many of us living in an instant world - we expect results and changes instantaneously. Don't get discouraged. Know that your hard work is paying off, even if you don't see the results you're looking for on the scale of right away.

—

What Can I Do About Muscle Soreness?

+

When it comes to muscle soreness, a few things will factor in to help you recover your best. First daily in your nutrition is key, eating foods to fuel your body and workouts is important as this will give your muscles the nutrients they need for recovery. Rest and plenty of sleep will also play into your recovery as well.

—

How Often Should I Rest?

+

Your rest depend on your workout schedule. Like I said previously, I recommend 1 or 2 full day rest in your schedule. This isn't only great for your body time to recover for your workouts, it also can be a great time for self-care to promote mental health.
